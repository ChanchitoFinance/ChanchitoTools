Why do solo developers hit walls that community-backed developers never encounter?

The pattern is clear: solo work relies on one perspective. Your perspective. When you're stuck, you're stuck with your own thinking. The problem looks unsolvable because you can't see beyond your current approach.

Community work multiplies perspectives. Every person brings a different viewpoint. Someone asks a question you never considered. Someone shares an approach you'd never thought of. Someone points out an assumption you didn't know you were making.

The difference isn't just about getting answers. It's about seeing problems differently. Solo work traps you in your perspective. Community work breaks you free.

When you're working alone, you're limited by what you can see. When you're working in community, you're limited by what the community can see. And communities see more.

The principle is simple: perspective is the constraint. Community expands that constraint. Solo work doesn't.

Consider your current approach. Are you stuck because the problem is hard, or because you're only seeing it from one angle?
